Developer Experience

In my short time in the industry, I have done my best to be a utility player. Amongst other things, I have written code, orchestrated test frameworks, set up DevOps infrastructures for large teams of over 100 engineers, spearheaded customer communications, interviewed and mentored other engineers, and helped to architect new features.

I thrive on tough challenges and adding new skills to my toolbelt. Never afraid to say I don't know something, I am on the constant quest for new knowledge. While working in a start up team, I was overjoyed to try new parts of the development process, whether there was a defined path or I had to find my own way.

Starting as a co-op for Rockwell Automation, I helped lead a team to create a continuous integration and testing framework for a team of over 100 engineers using Powershell, Jenkins and Hypervisor. Our finished product reduced automated testing cycles from 27 hours down to 2 hours and significantly reduced build times by building in parallel. For our efforts to increase efficiency and helping engineers feel secure with potentially breaking changes, we received internal recognition. Following this project, I also took a full feature from design through the software development process using C++.

After finishing my Bachelor's Degree at The Ohio State University, I returned to Rockwell Automation as a full time Associate Software Engineer. During my time in this position, I wrote new features, recruited and mentored other engineers, ran reading groups, and monitored test results. Along with this, I worked with a team to integrate an entirely new UI onto a legacy backend, ensuring that functionality and tests all remained working as expected.

After being promoted to a Software Engineer, I was recruited to another team in the company, which essentially functioned as a start up team within Rockwell Automation using a mix of Kanban and Scrum practices. With this being a three person team, my love for wearing multiple hats and constantly being challenged was able to flourish. I started with the team by creating the DevOps configuration for the project. As a team, we created microservices running on a controller using Python and C++. After the DevOps configuration, I helped to build the test infrastructure, built functionality in the micro services, took part in architecting new features and implementations. Later in the process, we added a UI using Node.Js, Angular, and Redis. Once the project was in beta, I was able to champion working with beta customers, taking feedback, helping with issues and adding stories to our board based on the interactions. While presenting the project at a conference, I was also able to partake in User Testing, to see how the UX teams made decisions for the UI. This role also included technical interviews of potential additions to the team and truly helped me grow as a developer.

Since leaving the team to explore what my next opportunity is and travel around the world, I have been searching to find what I want to work on next. With the freedom to explore new paths, I have been learning web development on my own, as I have had limited exposure to that area in my professional past. Using the plethora of information on the internet, I have been learning Ruby, Rails and React and a link to that project will be present on this site once it is in a more completed form. Work has also begun on a project using the Unity engine.

Personal Projects

King-Me, King/Queen of the Beach tournament generator

Tools/Tech: Ruby, Rails, AWS, Heroku, Docker, Postgresql, Bootstrap, RSpec, Git, Github Projects

King-Me is still a work in progress, but it is my first solo attempt at a sophisticated web application. Through this site, I taught myself to use all of the tools involved. The site automates all of the logic behind running a king/queen of the beach volleyball tournament, only requiring the user to input a seeded player list and scores. Ranking, progression and match generation is all handled by the backend logic.

Outside Of Code

As much as I love to code, I like to do a ton of other things. Learning new things is my favorite thing in the world, but some of those things end up becoming mainstays. For the most part, those are travel, volleyball, and writing.

For the past 6 months, I have been traveling through Asia and Europe, putting my total countries visited to 20 since I started traveling in 2016. Throughout those travels, I have picked up many languages, made a ton of friends and learned about many different cultures.

Also in 2016, I started playing competitive beach volleyball. I have played in many leagues and tournaments in Cleveland, Ohio with varying levels of success, as well as playing in 9 other states and 7 other countries. Usually while traveling, I will find somewhere to play and make new friends from all over the world. While in Thailand, I got to train with some players who have played in the Olympics and ended up becoming friends with them.

When I started traveling last year, I began keeping my friends and family updated with what I was up to by keeping up with blog posts. You can find those posts at NeverEverBeBoring.com and I plan to do some writing elsewhere on the internet when I return home. I always enjoyed writing but it's something I haven't done since the beginning of college.
